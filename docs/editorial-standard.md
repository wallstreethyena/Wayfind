# The Wayfind Editorial Standard (Atlas-590 v1)

This is THE rule. Every editorial on every card, page, and detail view — current and future — is written to
this standard. It is stored once, referenced everywhere, and never re-invented per page.

## The core law: translate the numbers, never recite them
Google's numbers are EVIDENCE, not the story. The reader must understand WHY a place earns its rank from
the prose itself. The score confirms what the words already made them feel.

- BANNED as the reason: "4.8★ across 6,058 reviews — a proven local favorite." (That's Google talking.)
- WAYFIND: "The quartz sand really does stay cool under your feet in August — and the thousands of people
  who've tested that claim agree with each other at a rate almost no beach sustains."
  (Same data. Now it's a reason.)

Numbers may appear, but only as the closing punch of a concrete claim — never as the claim itself.

## Voice
Confident local friend. Present tense. Second person where natural. Specific and sensory over general and
promotional. Short sentences win.

BANNED WORDS/MOVES: "hidden gem," "nestled," "boasts," "stunning," "must-see," "something for everyone,"
"look no further," exclamation marks, any superlative without a verifiable source, any AI/algorithm talk.

## Honesty (non-negotiable, inherited from the data-honesty rule)
- Every factual claim must be verifiable, with a source recorded.
- If a fact can't be verified, it is OMITTED — never softened, never guessed.
- Flaws are stated plainly when true (parking is brutal, weekends are packed) — honesty about downsides is
  what makes the praise believable.
- Nothing is written to please a venue. No paid placement, ever, and the prose never reads like it.

## The six fields (every card gets exactly these)
1. **hook** (≤120 chars) — the one thing that makes this place different from every neighbor. Specific,
   sensory, verifiable. This is the card's subtitle.
2. **why_here** (2–3 sentences) — why it's ranked where it is, per the core law above. This is the body.
3. **know_before** (1–2 sentences) — parking, fees, timing, the practical friction, stated straight.
4. **best_time** (1 sentence) — when to actually go, and why that hour/day.
5. **local_tip** (1 sentence) — one thing only someone who's been there repeatedly would know.
6. **facts** — 2–4 claims, each with a source URL. These back the prose; unbacked prose gets cut.

## Beaches additionally get (beach_extras)
- sand_type (verified: quartz, shell, coquina...), typical_water character,
- flag_system: which county/lifeguard flag system the beach uses and where the flag is posted,
- water_quality_program: the FL Healthy Beaches sampling station name for this beach (bacteria testing),
  if one exists.
Live values (water temp, wind, current flag color, latest bacteria result) come from live sources at
render time — the editorial stores the STABLE truth (which systems apply and where to look).

## Length discipline
Hook ≤120 chars. why_here ≤65 words. know_before ≤35 words. best_time ≤20 words. local_tip ≤25 words.
Tight is the brand.

## Test before publishing (all four must pass)
1. Could this exact paragraph be written about a different place? → rewrite until no.
2. Does the reader learn WHY it ranks here without needing the number? → the core law.
3. Would a local nod at the tip, or roll their eyes? → the tip test.
4. Is every claim sourced or cut? → the honesty gate.
